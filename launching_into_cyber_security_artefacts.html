LAUNCHING INTO CYBER Security

Below are a compilation of posts and exercises I have completed during this module.

INITIAL POST

Discuss why Cyber Security is now a global issue and why it is important for companies to invest in Cyber Security.

The internet has become a key resource in society, which has allowed globalisation to progress at an exponential rate. Cremer, Sheehan, Fortmann et al. (2022) found in 2020, cybercrime is calculated to cost the global economy nearly $1 trillion USD (£872. 4 billion), an increase of more than 50% from 2018.

A company affected by a cyber attack was TalkTalk in 2015. TalkTalk Telecom Group PLC provides broadband, TV and mobile services to over 95% of the UK population, from businesses to consumers. The telecoms giant was at the centre of a data breach, exposing sensitive customer information via a SQL injection. Over 150,000 accounts were compromised, including bank details, account numbers and sort codes. In 2009, TalkTalk took over the Italian telecommunications company, Tiscali in 2009. Their system used an old form of code that communicated with the database, which was the main cause of how the breach took place (Wall & Porcedda, 2019).

As a result, TalkTalk was penalised £400,000 by the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) for improper security measures as well as a further £100,000 in 2017 when data was exposed by three employees at Wipro, who TalkTalk was outsourcing work to (BBC News, 2016). This event was a detriment to the company's reputation, resulting in a parliamentary investigation into cybersecurity as well as the CEO at the time resigning (Pownall, 2020).

TalkTalk’s data breach shows why cybersecurity and data protection practices are important. Cyberculture is evolving faster than the technologies that protect it, so private data, intellectual property, and resources are at risk of being compromised or damaged by cyber attacks.
