    REFLECTIVE PIECE

    Network Security

    Below is a reflection of the work and exercises I completedover the course of this module.

    Under the practice of cybersecurity, network security is undoubtedly integral to the progression and safeguarding of sensitive data. Network security maintains the stability and dependability of your network by inspecting any sudden requests that may disrupt its functioning (ECPI University, n.d.). In today's digital age, data breaches are becoming increasingly common, and legal frameworks exist to protect sensitive information and their systems.

    This module has taught me how to scan websites for vulnerabilities and how to configure reports that are actionable with solid reasoning for why it is taking place. The various security standards such as ISO 27001 and 22301 has been insightful, as well as my independent research into the Computer Misuse Act 1990. This was implemented in UK law after cybercriminals gained access to sensitive information from a large telecoms company (National Crime Agency, n.d.) Renowned companies such as TalkTalk, which had improper infrastructure to protect sensitive data leading to large fines. I have further insight into the role of legal compliance in ensuring network security and understand the importance of adhering to these regulations to avoid legal consequences.

    When I begin my career as an Information Security professional, it is my imperative (and should be for Information Security personnel universally speaking), to prioritise the data of customers and organisations. It can be challenging at times to settle on a meshing of interests and demands between owners, shareholders and customers. This is why I truly value open communication in an environment where it can be stressful and there can be a lot at stake for upholding your personal reputation and the company’s. As someone who learns practically and also from their mistakes, having training and communication in a workplace is integral to growth, confidence and presenting human errors that may be the cause of cybersecurity breaches.

    One of the challenges I faced was during the executive summary where I had some issues trying to scan the domain through the Mac OS Terminal program to carry out a Tracert scan. It showed the message “no command found” which made me feel slightly defeated and lost. As I don’t come from a Computer Science background, it has been difficult. I try not to dwell on the fact that I don’t always grasp things straight away. The way I manage this and have managed this since the start of my degree is to approach each module with an open mind, with curiosity and positivity.

    Throughout the course, I have also explored various techniques and tools used in network security. The unit of interest for me was Unit 1, where I learned about the history of network security, the processes, and what can compromise this. With the threat-modelling technique, the Cyber Kill Chain. This procedure explains the process of how hackers perform a successful cyber attack. Developed by Lockheed Smith it has transcended the cybersecurity industry. One piece of the feedback I received was to evaluate it against the STRIDE threat model (Spoofing, Tampering, Repudiation, Information Disclosure, Denial of Service, and Elevation of Privilege) in order to strengthen my argument, for instance:

    In the first phase of a security breach, reconnaissance, attackers take every measure necessary to find out as much as possible about their target in order to identify vulnerabilities and ways in which access can be gained. Potential STRIDE threats are information exposure and fraudulent representation, wherein adversaries assume a false identity with the purpose of accessing data they otherwise could not touch (spoofing).

    This feedback made me realise that I need to analyse my work deeper instead of at face value.

    Despite the necessity and demands of network security, the wider debate is what is the future of it. When reading “A Survey on Future Internet Security Architectures” by Ding et al. (2016). When reading the section about MobilityFirst - a research project that can present data tampering and secure authentication as a way to new development of internet architecture. Further research into this, and I feel another argument is relevant here is AI technology. According to Choudhary (2020), it offers a sophisticated, versatile and developed. Also, they predict that product sales for cyber security could reach over 300 billion dollars. Cybersecurity professionals are in high demand as the digital landscape continues to evolve and expand.

    We are constantly connected to the internet and our electronic devices, using them for communication, learning, and browsing. This increased reliance on technology has created a growing need for cybersecurity professionals to tackle the rising security threats that come with the digital age. As the number of data breaches, ransomware attacks, and malicious software continues to rise, the need for qualified cybersecurity professionals to safeguard our data and networks is greater than ever.

    This module has been an incredibly valuable experience for me, teaching me the importance of being patient and inquisitive when it comes to learning. I have learned to go beyond the scope of what is expected of me, questioning my reasoning, and critically reflecting on the feedback I receive. I have also developed a greater appreciation for the importance of continuous learning and the value it brings to my personal and professional development. I am starting to approach tasks and problems with confidence and am eager to explore and develop as I learn throughout my degree.
